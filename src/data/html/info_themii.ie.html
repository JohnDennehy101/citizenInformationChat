About Mediation Overview Mediation offers a highly effective, real alternative to assist parties in resolving disputes.
The Benefits of Mediation Mediation can offer many benefits to parties in dispute including; avoiding costly legal/court services and the end-result may be agreed by the parties involved, instead of an external body.
The Mediation Process Each Mediation is different but certain factors are consistent in every case, such as an impartial Mediator, one to one and joint meetings with the Mediator and both parties.
Sectors of Mediation Mediation is proven to be a highly-effective method of dispute resolution across a very wide range of areas.
Information leaflets and videos The MII has produced some video and leaflet resources for your information.

About MII Overview The MII was founded on our belief in the practice of Mediation and the recognition that Ireland needed a professional body to raise awareness and standards in this emerging area.
Who We Are The MII Council is made up of members elected at the MII Annual General Meeting dedicated to the practice and development of quality mediation in Ireland.
Our Mission Our mission is to lead the continued development and promotion of Mediation services in Ireland.
A Framework for Excellence Our ‘Framework for Excellence’ assures that the the MII shall continue to promote excellence in the practice of Mediation.
The Green Pledge As a supporter of the World Mediators Alliance on Climate Change (WoMACC) Mediators’ Pledge, the MII invite all our members to become signatories.
Governance The MII is a not-for-profit organisation and a registered charity. Our governance documents outline the strands of the MII Governance process.
Code of Ethics and Practice Our Code of Ethics and Practice applies to all MII accredited Mediators.. It guides and supports Mediators, and they are obliged to comply with it.
President’s Letter Read our President’s letter.
Membership Overview The MII is the leading professional, not-for-profit association for Mediators in Ireland.
Types of Membership Learn about the different categories of membership offered by MII.
Recognition of Equivalent Training Process of application for recognition of equivalent training done with any training body other than one accredited by the MII.
Member Fees Registration fees are applicable on an annual calendar year basis (January-to-January) and should be submitted to the MII when making an online application for membership.
Reflective Practice and Learning Groups Providing an opportunity for between 6 and 12 Mediators to come together and share their mediation experience.

Accredited Mediation Training All training programmes approved by the Mediators’ Institute of Ireland are assessed against specific criteria based on the competencies required for the various levels of membership.

Elder Mediation Training MII recognises the importance of this unique growing area of mediation practice, and in responding to the demand for qualified Elder Mediators, providing a professional service, has established a specialist training programme for MII mediators wishing to work in this area.
Family (Separating Couples) accredited training 40 hours Separating Couples training is mandatory for members who wish to be certified to mediate with Separating Couples.
