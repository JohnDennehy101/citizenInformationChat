](/en/money-and-tax/tax/) > Tax Clearance Certificates # Tax Clearance Certificates * Introduction * When do I need a Tax Clearance Certificate? * How to apply ## Introduction A Tax Clearance Certificate is confirmation that your tax affairs are correct and up to date. You may need a Tax Clearance Certificate: * To apply for certain schemes or licences * If you get certain public sector payments * If you an office holder under Standards in Public Office laws You can apply for a Tax Clearance Certificate from Revenue. ## When do I need a Tax Clearance Certificate? You need a Tax Clearance Certificate to apply for some schemes and licences, including the: * [ Wage Subsidy Scheme for people with disabilities ](/en/employment/employment-and-disability/wage-subsidy-scheme-for-people-with-disabilities/) * [ Charities VAT Compensation Scheme ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/companies-and-charities/charities-and-sports-bodies/vat-compensation-scheme/vat-compensation-scheme-for-charities/index.aspx) * [ Small Public Service Vehicle Licence ](/en/travel-and-recreation/public-transport/regulation-of-taxis-and-small-public-service-vehicles/) [ View a list of other schemes and licences that require a Tax Clearance Certificate ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/starting-a-business/tax- clearance/when-is-a-tax-clearance-certificate-required/to-apply-for-or-renew- your-licence-or-scheme.aspx) . You also need a Tax Clearance Certificate if you get more than €10,000 in a year in: * Grants, subsidies or similar payments from public sector bodies * Payments from a public sector contract (inclusive of Value Added Tax) Some people are required to have a [ Tax Clearance Certificate under the Standards in Public Office laws ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/starting-a- business/tax-clearance/standards-in-public-office/index.aspx) . This includes: * Members of the Oireachtas * Senior public officials * Candidates for judicial appointments ## How to apply Make sure that all of your tax affairs are fully up to date before you apply. Revenue may issue a Tax Clearance Certificate where there are tax arrears if you have agreed an instalment arrangement to pay the arrears. Revenue will also check your [ relevant connected parties ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/starting-a-business/tax-clearance/connected- persons/index.aspx) . These include your properties and any business partnerships that you are a member of. You can apply for a Tax Clearance Certificate online using the electronic Tax Clearance (eTC) system. You can access the eTC in: * [ myAccount ](https://www.ros.ie/myaccount-web/home.html) – click 'Tax Clearance' on the 'Manage My Record' card * [ Revenue Online Service (ROS) ](https://www.ros.ie/ros-registration-web/ros-registration) – click ‘Manage Tax Clearance’ in the ‘My Services’ section [ Read more about how to apply for a Tax Clearance Certificate online ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/online-services/services/manage-your-record/apply- for-tax-clearance-online-using-etc.aspx) . If you cannot apply online, you can apply for a Tax Clearance Certificate by completing [ Form TC1 (pdf) ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/starting-a- business/documents/form-tc1.pdf) and sending the form to the [ Customer Services Unit, Collector-General's Division ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/contact-us/customer-service-contact/collector- generals-division.aspx) . There is a different application process if you need to [ apply for a Tax Clearance Certificate under the Standards in Public Office (SIPO) rules ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/starting-a-business/tax-clearance/standards-in- public-office/index.aspx) . **Appeals** If your application is refused, or you have a Tax Clearance Certificate withdrawn, you can appeal the decision within 30 days. The refusal or withdrawal letter that you get will list any tax or duty you owe.
