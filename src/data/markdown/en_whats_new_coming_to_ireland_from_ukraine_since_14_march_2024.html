**English** | Gaeilge **You are here:** [ Home ](/en/) > [ What's New ](/en/whats-new/) > Coming to Ireland from Ukraine since 14 March 2024 # Coming to Ireland from Ukraine since 14 March 2024 21 March 2024 ##### Site updates Since 14 March 2024, people [ coming to Ireland from Ukraine ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and- refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/coming-to-ireland-from-ukraine/) who register for [ temporary protection ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and- refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/temporary-protection-directive/) and State-provided accommodation, can be accommodated for a maximum of 90 days in a [ Designated Accommodation Centre ](https://www.gov.ie/en/press- release/54291-changes-to-accommodation-for-people-fleeing-war-in-ukraine/) . A [ Beneficiary of Temporary Protection Weekly Payment ](https://www.gov.ie/en/service/894dd-beneficiary-of-temporary-protection- weekly-payment/) of €38.80 for an adult and €29.80 for a child will be paid. A monthly [ Child Benefit ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social- welfare/social-welfare-payments/families-and-children/child-benefit/) payment will also be paid for qualifying children. Beneficiaries of Temporary Protection who are not in State-provided accommodation can apply for social welfare payments in the same way as Irish citizens if they meet the eligibility conditions. People from Ukraine who arrived in Ireland before 14 March 2024 and registered for temporary protection are not affected by these changes.
