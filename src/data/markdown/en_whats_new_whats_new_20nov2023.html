New 20 November 2023 ##### Site updates Some once-off cost of living payments announced in [ Budget 2024 ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/money-and-tax/budgets/budget-2024/) will be paid this week: * If you get [ Fuel Allowance ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/social-welfare-payments/extra-social-welfare-benefits/fuel-allowance/) , you will get a lump sum of €300 – the exact payment date varies per person * If you get [ Working Family Payment ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/social-welfare-payments/families-and-children/working-family-payment/) , you will get a lump sum of €400 on 23 November 2023 * If you get [ Disability Allowance ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/social-welfare-payments/disability-and-illness/disability-allowance/) , [ Invalidity Pension ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/social-welfare-payments/disability-and-illness/invalidity-pension/) and [ Blind Pension ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/social-welfare-payments/disability-and-illness/blind-persons-pension/) , you will get a lump sum of €400 between 22 and 24 November 2023 (you get the once-off payment on the same day you get your primary payment) To request a late student grant application, you must [ complete a Late Application Form 2023-2024 ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/education/third-level-education/fees- and-supports-for-third-level-education/student-grant-scheme/#4d7e38) and email it to [ lateapplications@susi.ie ](mailto:lateapplications@susi.ie) . Late applications will only be accepted in certain circumstances. People who have a submeter are now eligible for the [ Electricity Account Credit ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/consumer/utilities/electricity- account-credit/#31d1bf) . Previously, households with submeters were not eligible (submeters are linked to a primary meter with an MPRN). A new [ low usage threshold applies ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/consumer/utilities/electricity- account-credit/#33b71c) where households that use less than 150kWh a quarter in 4 consecutive quarters will not be eligible for the credit. People from Ukraine covered by the Temporary Protection Directive can take [ Christmas leave from their accommodation ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and- refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/coming-to-ireland-from-ukraine/#4583cd) without needing to request an absence. Christmas absences run from 22 December 2023 to 8 January 2024. On 13 November 2023, new regulations came into effect on [ monitoring and treatment of sex offenders in Ireland ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/justice/criminal-law/criminal- trial/sex-offenders-register/) . The changes: * Reduce the notification period for sex offenders to inform Gardaí of changes to personal information (such as change of address) from 7 days to 3 days * Allow Gardaí to take fingerprints, palm-prints or photographs to confirm the identity of an offender * Allow Gardaí to disclose information about people on the sex offender register where there is a serious risk to the public * Ensure that the courts can stop an offender from working with children The [ Local Authority Home Loan ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/housing/owning-a-home/help-with- buying-a-home/local-authority-mortgages/) is due to be extended in 2024, so you will be able to use it to buy and renovate derelict and non-habitable homes. From 1 December 2023, social housing tenants who [ rent a room in their home ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/housing/owning-a-home/home- owners/renting-a-room-in-your-home/) to a third-level student qualify for rent-a-room relief. Scholarship payments under UCD’s Cothrom na Féine Scholarship Programme of up to €7,000 per year are not included in the [ means test ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare- system/means-test-for-social-welfare-payments/cash-income-not-included-in-the- social-welfare-means-test/) for most social welfare payments. From 1 January 2024, [ paid sick leave ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/employment-rights-and- conditions/leave-and-holidays/sick-leave-and-sick-pay/) increases from 3 to 5 days a year. **New and recently edited** New pages: * [ Long-term Carers Contributions
