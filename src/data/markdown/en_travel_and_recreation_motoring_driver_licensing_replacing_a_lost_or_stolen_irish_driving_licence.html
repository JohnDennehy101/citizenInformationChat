learner permit # Replacing a lost, stolen or damaged driving licence or learner permit * Introduction * Cost of replacing your driving licence or permit * Apply online * Apply in person * More information ## Introduction If you need a replacement driving licence, apply to the National Driver Licence Service (NDLS). The replacement will be a copy of your original and valid for the same period. If you had a [ special driving licence code ](https://www.ndls.ie/about/licence-categories-and-codes.html#licence-codes) , for example, you must wear glasses when driving, it will still be there. The Road Safety Authority has responsibility for driver licensing. The National Driver Licence Service (NDLS) manages licence applications and renewals. You can apply online, which is the quickest way, or in person at an NDLS office. ## Cost of replacing your driving licence or permit The fee for a replacement driving licence or learner permit is €35. The replacement fee of €35 applies to the over 70’s if the licence is lost or damaged. If you are paying online, you can pay by credit or debit card. ## Apply online You can apply online which is the quickest way. You will need the following to apply online: * A [ Public Services Card ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/public-services-card/) and verified [ MyGovID ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/government-in-ireland/how-government-works/egovernment/mygovid/) * A completed [ Garda Declaration Form ](https://www.ndls.ie/images/Documents/169620_RSA_NDLS_Garda_Declaration_Form_Web_HR.pdf) * [ Proof of your address ](https://www.ndls.ie/how-to-apply/applying-in-person.html#evidence-of-address) dated within the last 6 months if your address is different to the one you provided when you got your Public Services Card or if your current address is not recorded on your licence * A [ medical report form ](https://www.ndls.ie/images/Documents/Forms/171315_NDLS_Medical_Form_JAN_2022_WEB_HR.pdf) dated within one month, if there has been a change in your medical details since you made your last application for your driving licence. * Application fee of €35 ## Apply in person You must [ book an appointment ](https://www.ndls.ie/booking-service.html) . There is no walk-in service available. You must bring the following documentation: * A completed [ Garda Declaration Form ](https://www.ndls.ie/images/Documents/169620_RSA_NDLS_Garda_Declaration_Form_Web_HR.pdf) * Documentation to [ prove your identity ](https://www.ndls.ie/how-to-apply/applying-in-person.html#photo-id) * Proof of your [ personal public services number ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/personal-public-service-number/) (PPSN) * [ Proof of address ](https://www.ndls.ie/how-to-apply/applying-in-person.html#evidence-of-address) dated within the last 6 months if your address is different to the one you provided when you got your Public Services Card or if your current address is not recorded on your licence * A [ medical report form ](https://www.ndls.ie/images/Documents/Forms/171315_NDLS_Medical_Form_JAN_2022_WEB_HR.pdf) dated within one month, if there has been a change in your medical details since you made your last application for your driving licence. * Application fee of €35 ## More information If your licence has expired, you can find out [ how to renew your licence ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/travel-and-recreation/motoring/driver- licensing/renewing-a-full-driving-licence/) . See all our information on [ driver licensing ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/travel-and- recreation/motoring/driver-licensing/) .
