calculating your redundancy payment * Introduction ## Introduction You can use a [ redundancy calculator on MyWelfare.ie ](https://www.mywelfare.ie/redundancycalculator) to estimate your statutory redundancy entitlements. Date your employment terminates (for example, 30 June 2021) ------------ Date you started work (for example, 1 April 2011) -------------- Number of full years' service (for example, 10 years) -------------- Number of days in part of a year's service (total of days in every incomplete year worked for example, 1 April 2021 to 30 June 2021 = 3 months = 91 days) \--------------- Deduct any non-reckonable service (see [ Redundancy payments ](/en/employment/unemployment-and- redundancy/redundancy/redundancy-payments/) for details for example, 30 weeks' sick leave in 2012, 4 weeks is non-reckonable. 4 weeks = 28 days) ---------------- Total of the days in a part year as percentage of a year (for example, 63 ÷ 365 x 100 = 17.26%) ---------------- Total amount of service (for example, 10.17 years) ---------------- Amount of your normal weekly pay (gross pay, that is, before tax and PRSI deducted for example, €700, ceiling on weekly pay is €600) ---------------- Statutory redundancy lump sum payment due (2 weeks' pay for each year of service + one extra week for example, 2 weeks x 10.17 = 20.34 + 1 week = 21.34 weeks. Example lump sum due is €12.804 (€600 x 21.34) ---------------- You can see [ examples of redundancy calculations on gov.ie ](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/4be20-redundancy-calculation-
