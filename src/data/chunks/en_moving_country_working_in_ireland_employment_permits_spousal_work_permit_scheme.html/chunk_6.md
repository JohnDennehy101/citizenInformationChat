##  Immigration rules

If your family is from a country that needs a [ visa to travel to Ireland
](/en/moving-country/visas-for-ireland/visa-requirements-for-entering-
ireland/) , each family member must apply for their own visa. You can read
guidance on the [ documents you must provide
](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-join-family-in-ireland/) with the
application.

If your family is from a country that does not need a visa, then they can
travel to Ireland and [ present documentation
](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-join-family-in-ireland/) to an
immigration officer at the airport or port.

De-facto partners (and their dependent children) of Critical Skills Employment
Permit or Hosting Agreement holders who are from a country that does not need
a visa, must [ apply for pre-clearance
](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-join-family-in-ireland/joining-an-
eea-or-swiss-national/de-facto-partner-of-a-critical-skills-employment-permit-
holder-csep-or-hosting-agreement-holder-ha/) before coming to Ireland. A de-
facto partner is a person that you are in a committed relationship with that
is like a marriage, but you are not actually married.

**After arriving in Ireland** : Your spouse or de-facto partner and any child
aged 16 and over must [ register and get an Irish Residence Permit (IRP)
](/en/moving-country/moving-to-ireland/rights-of-residence-in-
ireland/registration-of-non-eea-nationals-in-ireland/) .
