##  What is the Temporary Protection Directive?

The [ Temporary Protection Directive ](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-
content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32001L0055&qid=1648223587338) is an EU law that
was introduced in 2001. The Directive created a special procedure to deal with
a ‘mass influx’ of people in need of international protection. The Directive
must be proposed by the [ EU Commission ](/en/government-in-ireland/european-
government/eu-institutions/european-commission/) and accepted by a vote of the
[ European Council ](/en/government-in-ireland/european-government/eu-
institutions/european-council/) .

The Directive was adopted into Irish law by [ Section 60 of the International
Protection Act 2015
](https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2015/act/66/section/60/enacted/en/html#sec60)
.

The [ Directive was activated ](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-
content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A52022XC0321%2803%29&qid=1647940863274) for the
first time in March 2022 in [ response to the war in Ukraine ](/en/moving-
country/asylum-seekers-and-refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/coming-to-
ireland-from-ukraine/) . It has been extended to March 2026.
