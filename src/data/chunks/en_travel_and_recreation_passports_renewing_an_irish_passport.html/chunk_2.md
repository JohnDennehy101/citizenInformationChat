##  Introduction

This page explains how to renew your [ Irish passport ](/en/travel-and-
recreation/passports/irish-passports-an-overview/) if you are over 18 years
old.

You **must be an** [ **Irish citizen** ](/en/moving-country/irish-
citizenship/your-right-to-irish-citizenship/) to be eligible for an Irish
passport.

Irish passports for adults are valid for 10 years. To continue to travel
abroad after the time expires, you must apply to renew your Irish passport.
You may also want to apply to renew your passport because:

  * Your passport was [ lost, stolen or damaged ](/en/travel-and-recreation/passports/replacing-a-lost-or-stolen-irish-passport/)
  * You have [ changed your name ](/en/travel-and-recreation/passports/renewing-an-irish-passport#lc8a05)

If your most recent passport was issued [ more than 15 years ago
](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/passports/documentary-requirements/adult/) ,
you must apply as a [ first time applicant
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/travel-and-
recreation/passports/applying-for-or-renewing-an-irish-passport/) .

You can renew your passport at any time. If you apply for a new passport
before your current one has expired, your new passport will run from when you
apply.

**Three ways to renew your Irish passport:**

  * Online, through [ Passport Online ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/passports/passport-online/)
  * By post, through [ Post Passport ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/passports/post-passport/)
