###  **Returning with nowhere to stay on arrival**

If you are [ homeless ](/en/returning-to-ireland/finding-somewhere-to-
live/guide-to-housing-supports-for-returning-irish-emigrants/) when you return
to Ireland, you should contact the [ local authority
](https://www.housing.gov.ie/local-government/administration/local-
authorities/local-authorities) in the area that you have a [ local connection
](/en/returning-to-ireland/finding-somewhere-to-live/guide-to-housing-
supports-for-returning-irish-emigrants#l80745) to apply for emergency [
accommodation assistance ](/en/housing/losing-your-home/agencies-for-homeless-
people#l3e1b6) . As part of the assessment, you must [ apply for social
housing ](/en/housing/local-authority-and-social-housing/applying-for-local-
authority-housing/) .

homeless freephone on 1800 707 707 (2pm to 2am Monday to Friday and 10am to
2am weekends and Bank Holidays). Visit [ homelessdublin.ie
](https://www.homelessdublin.ie/) for more information.

There are also day service supports available in urban parts of Ireland such
as Dublin and Cork from [ agencies providing services for homeless people
](/en/housing/losing-your-home-and-homelessness/agencies-for-homeless-people/)
. Many of these services provide showers, lockers and mental health supports.
