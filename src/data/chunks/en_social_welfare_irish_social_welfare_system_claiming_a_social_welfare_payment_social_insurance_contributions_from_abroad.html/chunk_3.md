##  UK and combining social insurance

On 31 January 2020, the UK left the EU - commonly known as Brexit. The [
Convention on Social Security between Ireland and the United Kingdom (pdf)
](http://opac.oireachtas.ie/AWData/Library3/EASPdoclaid07022019_095636.pdf)
was agreed to ensure that:

  * Irish citizens living in Ireland can still benefit from social insurance contributions made while working in the UK, and vice versa 
  * EU citizens living in Ireland can use their social insurance contributions made when working in the UK to help them qualify for certain Irish social welfare payments 
  * Existing payments continue to be paid as before. You can find a [ list of Irish social welfare payments covered by the Convention ](https://www.gov.ie/en/press-release/c1896-social-protection-measures-between-ireland-and-the-united-kingdom-are-maintained/)
