###  Redundancy

If you are dismissed by your employer you should check if it is a [ redundancy
situation, ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/unemployment-
and-redundancy/redundancy/what-is-redundancy/) for example, the closure of a
business. Your employer must prove that there was a genuine redundancy
situation and that [ fair procedures were followed.
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/unemployment-and-
redundancy/dismissal/fair-grounds-for-dismissal/) If it is not a genuine
redundancy or if you think you have been unfairly selected for redundancy, you
may qualify to bring a claim for [ unfair dismissal.
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/unemployment-and-
redundancy/dismissal/unfair-dismissal/)

If it is a genuine redundancy situation and you have been working for your
employer for at least 2 years, you may be entitled to a [ redundancy payment
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/unemployment-and-
redundancy/redundancy/redundancy-payments/) .

You may have [ other entitlements such as notice and holiday pay
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/unemployment-and-
redundancy/losing-your-job/losing-job-entitlements/) .
