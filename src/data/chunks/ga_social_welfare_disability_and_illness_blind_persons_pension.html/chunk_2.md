##  What is the Blind Pension?

The Blind Pension is a means-tested payment paid to people who are blind or
have low vision and who are [ habitually resident
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-
system/social-assistance-payments/habitual-residence-condition/) in Ireland.
It is paid by the Department of Social Protection.

The payment is made up of a personal rate for you, and extra amounts for a
qualified adult (such as your spouse) and any child dependants (see 'Rate of
Blind Pension' below).

If you get a Blind Pension, you may also qualify for the [ Blind Welfare
Allowance paid by the HSE
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/health/health-services/health-
services-for-people-with-disabilities/blind-welfare-allowance/) .

If you get Blind Pension and another source of income, you may have to pay
tax. Read about [ taxation of social welfare payments
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-
system/claiming-a-social-welfare-payment/taxation-of-social-welfare-payments/)
.
