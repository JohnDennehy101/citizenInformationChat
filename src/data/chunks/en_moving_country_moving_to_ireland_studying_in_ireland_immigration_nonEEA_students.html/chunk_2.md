##  Studying in Ireland

Non-EEA nationals coming to study in Ireland must be enrolled in an eligible
full-time course. You can read a list of [ frequently asked questions
](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-study-in-ireland/frequently-asked-
questions-for-students/) for non-EEA students on the website of Immigration
Service Delivery (ISD).

Students who are from a country that [ requires a visa to enter Ireland
](/en/moving-country/visas-for-ireland/visa-requirements-for-entering-
ireland/) , must apply for a student visa. Their visa application must include
[ evidence that they have access ](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-
study-in-ireland/what-are-my-study-options/a-fee-paying-private-primary-or-
secondary-school/information-on-student-finances/) to €10,000 when they come
to Ireland.

All non-EEA students, including those who do not require a visa, must [
register with their local immigration officer ](/en/moving-country/moving-to-
ireland/rights-of-residence-in-ireland/registration-of-non-eea-nationals-in-
ireland/) to get permission to remain in Ireland for more than 90 days.
Students who plan to study for up to 6 months must have access to €700 per
month or €4,200 (whichever is the lesser) when they first register with their
local immigration officer.
