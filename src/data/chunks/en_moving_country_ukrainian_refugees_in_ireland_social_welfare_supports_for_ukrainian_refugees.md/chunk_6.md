###  From 14 March 2024

From 14 March 2024, if you register for temporary protection and State-
provided accommodation, you will be accommodated for a maximum of 90 days in a
[ Designated Accommodation Centre ](https://www.gov.ie/en/press-
release/54291-changes-to-accommodation-for-people-fleeing-war-in-ukraine/) .

You will get a [ weekly payment ](https://www.gov.ie/en/service/894dd-
beneficiary-of-temporary-protection-weekly-payment/) of €38.80 for an adult
and €29.80 for a child. If you have any qualified children, you will also get
a monthly [ Child Benefit ](/en/social-welfare/families-and-children/child-
benefit/) payment.

You can read more about [ income supports and State-provided accommodation
](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/f011b-income-supports-for-those-arriving-
from-ukraine-under-the-eu-temporary-protection-directive/) .

If you are not in State-provided accommodation, you can apply for [ social
welfare payments ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/claiming-a-
social-welfare-payment/applying-for-a-social-welfare-payment/) in the same way
as Irish citizens, as long as you meet the eligibility conditions.
