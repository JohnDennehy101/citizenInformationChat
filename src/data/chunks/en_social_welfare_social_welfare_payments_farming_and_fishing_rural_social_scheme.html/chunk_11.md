##  Rates of payment for the Rural Social Scheme

The rates of payment for new participants on RSS depends on your qualifying
DSP payment. The minimum weekly payment increased to **€259.50 in January
2024.**

If the actual social welfare payment (including dependants) you were getting
is €232 a week or less, then you will get the minimum RSS weekly rate of
€259.50 (that is €232 plus €27.50). If your actual weekly social welfare
payment (including dependants) was €232.01 or more, then you will get the
equivalent rate plus €27.50.

If you were getting an age-related reduced rate of payment, your payment is
increased to the minimum weekly rate of €259.50.

**Weekly** **Rural Social Scheme rates 2024**

**Qualifying payment** |  **Rate of RSS payment**  
---|---  
Jobseeker's Allowance or Farm Assist  |  Equal to your current rate of payment plus €27.50 top-up. Minimum weekly payment of €259.50   
One-Parent Family Payment  |  Equal to your current rate of payment plus €27.50 top-up. Minimum weekly payment of €259.50.   
Disability Allowance  |  Equal to your current rate of payment plus €27.50 top-up. Minimum weekly payment of €259.50.   
Widow's, Widower's or Surviving Civil Partner's Contributory Pension  |  You continue to get your pension with a top-up of €27.50.   
Widow's, Widower's or Surviving Civil Partner's Non-Contributory Pension  |  You continue to get your pension with a top-up of €27.50 (The minimum combined payment is €259.50).   
Qualified Adult on a State Pension (Non-Contributory)  |  Your spouse will continue to get the Increase for a Qualified Adult (IQA). Your RSS rate will be the difference between your IQA payment and €259.50.   
  
**PRSI contribution**

A Class A PRSI contribution is paid on behalf of all RSS participants. You do
not have to pay [ Universal Social Charge (USC) ](/en/money-and-
tax/tax/income-tax/universal-social-charge/) on your RSS payment.

**Tax**

Your income is liable for tax. Depending on your circumstances, it is likely
you will pay little or no tax. Find out [ more about tax ](/en/money-and-
tax/tax/income-tax/) .

**Other social welfare payments**

If you are getting other social welfare payments you should check with your [
Intreo Centre or Social Welfare Branch Office
](https://www.gov.ie/en/directory/category/e1f4b5-intreo-offices/) to find out
whether they will be affected. You can keep your [ medical card
](/en/health/medical-cards-and-gp-visit-cards/medical-card/) while you are
taking part in the Rural Social Scheme.
