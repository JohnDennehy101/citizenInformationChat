##  How to apply

If you are coming to Ireland from a country outside the EU with â‚¬10,000 cash
or more, you must present a completed [ cash declaration form (pdf)
](https://revenue.ie/en/customs/individuals/customs/cash-declaration-form.pdf)
to the Customs at the airport or seaport when you enter Ireland. If you fail
to submit a declaration your cash may be detained by Customs and you could be
subject to penalties. [ There is further information about cash controls on
the Revenue website ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-free-
allowances/travelling-with-cash/index.aspx) .

You can find information on what goods you can bring with you for those [
travelling from the EU ](https://www.revenue.ie/en/importing-vehicles-duty-
free-allowances/customs-information-travelling-duty-free-
allowances/travelling-from-within-the-eu/duty-paid-and-tax-paid-goods.aspx)
and for those [ travelling from outside the EU
](https://www.revenue.ie/en/customs/individuals/info-travelling-duty-
free/from-outside-eu/index.aspx) on the Revenue website. If you are in any
doubt about the items you can or cannot import from within or outside the EU,
you should contact the Customs Information Office.
