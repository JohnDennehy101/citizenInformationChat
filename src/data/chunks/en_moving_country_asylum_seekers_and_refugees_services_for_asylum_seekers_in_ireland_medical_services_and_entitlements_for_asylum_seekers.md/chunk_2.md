##  Medical cards

Your [ local health centre
](http://www.hse.ie/eng/services/list/2/healthcentres/) or [ Local Health
Office ](http://www.hse.ie/eng/services/list/1/LHO/) can help you find a
general practitioner (GP) and give you a medical card application form.

The [ medical card ](/en/health/medical-cards-and-gp-visit-cards/medical-
card/) gives you and your family access to medical services free of charge. If
you do not apply for and receive a medical card, you will be asked to pay for
certain medical services. Services available free of charge or at low cost
under the Medical Card Scheme include:

  * [ General Practitioner ](/en/health/health-services/gp-and-hospital-services/gp-services-to-medical-card-holders/) (local doctor) services 
  * Hospital [ in-patient and out-patient services ](/en/health/health-services/gp-and-hospital-services/hospital-services-introduction/) (as a public patient) 
  * [ Prescription medicines ](/en/health/drugs-and-medicines/prescribed-drugs-and-medicines/) (with some exceptions) 
  * [ Women's health services ](/en/health/health-services/reproductive_health/)
  * Psychological services for people traumatised by torture, rape or other critical life experiences 
  * [ Sight tests ](/en/health/health-services/dental-aural-and-optical-services/sight-tests-and-eye-health/) and glasses 
  * [ Hearing tests ](/en/health/health-services/dental-aural-and-optical-services/hearing-and-ear-health/) and hearing aids 
  * [ Dental treatment ](/en/health/health-services/dental-aural-and-optical-services/dental-services/) for adults 
  * If your child is referred after a [ school medical examinations, ](/en/health/health-services/children-s-health/school-health-services/) dental treatment for children.
