##  Help available

Embassies and consulates can be of assistance if:

  * You need an [ emergency passport ](https://www.dfa.ie/travel/assistance-abroad/lost-or-stolen-passport-abroad/)
  * You have been the [ victim of crime ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/victim-of-crime/) or illness or have had a serious accident 
  * You have been [ arrested or imprisoned ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/arrest-detention/) while abroad 
  * You are facing [ deportation ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/deportation/)
  * You need help in transferring funds from home 
  * A family member or friend is [ missing abroad ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/missing-person-abroad/)
  * There is a [ death abroad ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/death-abroad/) and the remains need to be returned to Ireland. 

There is a limit to what the embassy or consulate can do. For example, if you
have been [ ill or in an accident ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-
travel/assistance-abroad/sick-or-injured/) , they can:

  * Contact your family or friends in Ireland 
  * Help you find English-speaking doctors, translators, and medical services 
  * Liaise with local medical services on your behalf 
  * Transfer funds from family and friends to you. 

However, they cannot pay your medical bills or other medical expenses. They
also cannot get involved in commercial or legal disputes, give legal advice or
pay your legal bills.
