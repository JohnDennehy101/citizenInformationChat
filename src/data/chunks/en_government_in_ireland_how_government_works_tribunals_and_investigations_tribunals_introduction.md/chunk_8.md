##  Bodies with specialised investigative powers

There is a range of specialised legislation regulating particular sectors,
such as the Companies Act 2014, which provide for investigations. The
following are some examples:

**Inquiries about** |  **Carried out by** |  **Legislation**  
---|---|---  
Railway accidents and incidents  |  [ Railway Accident Investigation Unit ](https://www.raiu.ie/) |  [ European Union (Railway Safety) (Reporting and Investigation of Serious Accidents, Accidents and Incidents) Regulations 2020 ](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2020/si/430/made/en/print)  
Marine casualties that take place in Irish waters, and accidents involving Irish registered vessels  |  [ Marine Casualty Investigation Board ](https://www.mcib.ie/) |  [ Merchant Shipping (Investigation of Marine Casualties) Act 2000 ](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2000/act/14/enacted/en/html) and associated regulations   
Aircraft accidents  |  [ Air Accident Investigation Unit ](http://www.aaiu.ie/) |  [ Air Navigation (Notification and Investigation of Accidents, Serious Accidents and Incidents) Regulations 2009 ](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2009/si/460/made/en/print)  
Companies  |  Inspectors appointed by a court  |  [ Part 13 of the Companies Act 2014 ](http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/2014/act/38/enacted/en/print#part13)
