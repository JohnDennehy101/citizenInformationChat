##  How much Class S PRSI do I pay?

You pay Class S PRSI of 4% on your **total income** for tax purposes. Your
total income is your gross income less [ allowable expenses
](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/294bc0-operational-guidelines-prsi-for-
the-self-employed/#:~:text=What%20is%20excluded%20from%20reckonable%20income)
.

You must pay 4% of your total income or €500, whichever is greater. From 1
October 2024, the minimum contribution will increase by €150 to €650.

If you have been told by an Inspector of Taxes that you do not need to make a
tax return, but you earn more than €5,000 in a year, you pay a flat rate
contribution of €310 to the Department of Social Protection.

If you earn less than €5,000 from self-employment in a year, you are exempt
from PRSI, but you may pay €500 as a [ voluntary contributor (if you meet the
other conditions) ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/social-
insurance-prsi/voluntary-prsi-contributions/) . From 1 October 2024, the
minimum contribution will increase by €150 to €650.

If you help your spouse or civil partner with running the family business, but
you are not their employee, you pay Class S contributions. Read more about [
employing family members and PRSI
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-
system/social-insurance-prsi/social-insurance/#90d8ef) .
