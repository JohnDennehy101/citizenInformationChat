###  How to apply

Before applying for a learner permit, you need to make sure you are eligible
to learn to drive in Ireland and have the documentation you need. To apply for
a learner permit for any vehicle, you must:

  * [ Be normally resident in Ireland ](https://www.ndls.ie/about-the-learner-permit)
  * [ Meet the minimum age requirement ](/en/travel-and-recreation/motoring/driver-licensing/categories-of-motor-vehicles-and-minimum-age-of-drivers-in-ireland/)
  * Have a [ driver theory test pass certificate ](https://www.rsa.ie/services/learner-drivers/the-driving-test/after-you-pass-your-test) (dated within 2 years of issue) 

To apply for your first learner permit, you can [ apply online
](https://ndls.rsa.ie/) or in person at an [ NDLS centre
](https://www.ndls.ie/where-to-apply.html) . You must make an appointment
first if you are applying in person.

You must also have passed an eye test for driving. You must submit a
satisfactory eyesight report (dated within one month) with your first learner
permit application. The eyesight report must be completed by a registered
optometrist or medical practitioner. Read more on the [ NDLS website
](https://www.ndls.ie/medical-fitness/do-i-need-to-submit-an-eyesight-report-
form.html) .

You can find out [ what other documentation you will need to apply on the NDLS
website ](https://www.ndls.ie/how-to-apply/learner-permit.html) .
