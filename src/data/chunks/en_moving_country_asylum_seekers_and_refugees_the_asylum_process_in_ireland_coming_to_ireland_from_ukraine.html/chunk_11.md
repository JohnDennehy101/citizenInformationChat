####  From 14 March 2024

If you register for temporary protection and State-provided accommodation at
any time from 14 March 2024, you will be accommodated for a maximum of 90 days
in State-provided accommodation, called a [ Designated Accommodation Centre
](https://www.gov.ie/en/press-release/54291-changes-to-accommodation-for-
people-fleeing-war-in-ukraine/) . You can read about the [ rules when staying
in State-provided accommodation
](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/62512-accommodation/) .

If you are in State-provided accommodation, you get a [ weekly payment
](https://www.gov.ie/en/service/894dd-beneficiary-of-temporary-protection-
weekly-payment/) of €38.80 for an adult and €29.80 for a child. If you have
any qualified children, you will also get a monthly [ Child Benefit
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/families-and-
children/child-benefit/) payment.

If you are not in State-provided accommodation, you can apply for social
welfare payments in the same way as Irish citizens, as long as you meet the
eligibility conditions.
