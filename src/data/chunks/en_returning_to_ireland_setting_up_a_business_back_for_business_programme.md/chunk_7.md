##  Further information

Visit the [ Back for Business website ](https://www.backforbusiness.com/) for
further information on the programme. Back for Business has also published a [
brochure on the programme (pdf) ](https://www.backforbusiness.com/wp-
content/uploads/2022/01/BfB-Brochure-Jan2022.pdf) and a [ summary of the
available supports for businesses (pdf) ](https://www.backforbusiness.com/wp-
content/uploads/2021/06/Back-for-Business-Booklet-2021.pdf) .

If you are thinking of [ setting up a business when you return to Ireland
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/returning_to_ireland/setting_up_a_business/)
, you can read about [ starting a business ](/en/employment/types-of-
employment/self-employment/starting-a-business/) and the regulations that
apply (for example, paying tax and hiring employees).

The Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment has an [ online guide to
the range of Government supports for start-ups and small businesses
](https://supportingsmes.gov.ie/sme-search/) .

Local Enterprise Offices provide supports such as advice and grants to local
businesses (10 or fewer employees) that are starting up or in development. You
can find information about their [ training programmes
](https://www.localenterprise.ie/Discover-Business-Supports/Training-
Programmes/) and [ start your own business courses
](https://www.localenterprise.ie/Discover-Business-Supports/Training-
Programmes/Start-Your-Own-Business-Programme/) as well as [ mentoring
](https://www.localenterprise.ie/Discover-Business-Supports/Mentoring/) and [
financial supports ](https://www.localenterprise.ie/Discover-Business-
Supports/Financial-Supports/) on [ localenterprise.ie
](https://www.localenterprise.ie/) .
