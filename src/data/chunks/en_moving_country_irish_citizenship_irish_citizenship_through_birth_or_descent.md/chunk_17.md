##  Further information and contacts

If you are entitled to citizenship by birth you can apply for an Irish
passport. You can read about:

  * [ How to apply for your first Irish passport as an adult ](/en/travel-and-recreation/passports/applying-for-or-renewing-an-irish-passport/)
  * [ Your child’s first Irish passport ](/en/travel-and-recreation/passports/passports-for-children/)
  * [ Irish passports – an overview ](/en/travel-and-recreation/passports/irish-passports-an-overview/)

If you were born abroad and your grandparents were born in Ireland, or your
parent or parents are Irish citizens who were not born in Ireland, you can
read about how to claim Irish citizenship through the [ Foreign Births
Register ](/en/moving-country/irish-citizenship/foreign-births-register/) .

If you are not an Irish citizen by birth and you live in Ireland, you can read
about how to qualify for [ Irish citizenship by naturalisation. ](/en/moving-
country/irish-citizenship/becoming-an-irish-citizen-through-naturalisation/)

To apply for a passport aboard, contact your nearest [ Irish embassy or
consulate ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/embassies/)
