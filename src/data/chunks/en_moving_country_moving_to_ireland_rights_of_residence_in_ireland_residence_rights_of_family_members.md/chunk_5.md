###  Partners

If you are [ returning to Ireland with your de facto non-EEA partner
](/en/returning-to-ireland/residency-and-citizenship/returning-to-ireland-
with-your-de-facto-partner/) , they must [ apply for a visa or preclearance
](/en/moving-country/visas-for-ireland/visa-requirements-for-entering-
ireland/) before coming to Ireland. A de facto partner is a person you are in
a relationship with that is like a marriage, but you are not married.

If you are in a de facto relationship with someone who is already resident in
Ireland (for example, if they have a student visa) they can [ apply for
residence based on their relationship ](https://www.irishimmigration.ie/my-
situation-has-changed-since-i-arrived-in-ireland/de-facto-partner-of-an-irish-
or-non-eea-national/#submit-application) with you. You are eligible to apply
if you have been in a relationship for 2 years or more.

Applications from de facto partners who are living in Ireland without a valid
residence permission in Ireland are not accepted.
