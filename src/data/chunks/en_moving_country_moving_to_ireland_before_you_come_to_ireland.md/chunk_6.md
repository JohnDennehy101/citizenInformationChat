##  General information about living in Ireland

**Returning to Ireland:** Our section on returning to Ireland has information
about what you should know if you are planning on returning to Ireland after
living abroad.

**Retiring in Ireland** : Find out about [ returning to Ireland to retire
](/en/returning-to-ireland/retiring-to-ireland/retiring-to-ireland-as-a-
returning-irish-emigrant/) , or [ moving to Ireland to retire ](/en/moving-
country/moving-to-ireland/coming-to-live-in-ireland/retiring-to-ireland/) .

**Government:** Ireland is a parliamentary democracy. All residents have the [
right to vote ](/en/government-in-ireland/elections-and-
referenda/voting/right-to-vote/) in [ local elections
](/government_in_ireland/elections_and_referenda/local_elections/) and EU
citizens also have the right to vote in [ European elections
](/government_in_ireland/elections_and_referenda/european_elections/) . UK
citizens have the right to vote in [ national elections ](/en/government-in-
ireland/elections-and-referenda/types-of-elections-and-referendums/) as well.
Read further information about the [ national government ](/en/government-in-
ireland/how-government-works/) and [ local government ](/en/government-in-
ireland/how-government-works/local-and-regional-government/functions-of-local-
authorities/) .

**Tax and social insurance contributions:** If you are working or self-
employed you must pay [ PRSI contributions ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-
welfare-system/social-insurance-prsi/social-insurance/) in the same way as
Irish people. If you are resident in Ireland, you may be liable for [ income
tax ](/en/money-and-tax/tax/income-tax/) and for [ capital taxes. ](/en/money-
and-tax/tax/capital-taxes/) There are specific rules about [ residency for tax
purposes ](/en/money-and-tax/tax/moving-country-and-taxation/tax-residence-
and-domicile-in-ireland/) .

**Education:** Free [ primary education ](/en/education/primary-and-post-
primary-education/going-to-primary-school/) and [ secondary education
](/en/education/primary-and-post-primary-education/going-to-post-primary-
school/) is available to all children aged under 18 legally resident in
Ireland. There are also some fee-paying primary and secondary schools
available. School attendance is mandatory until the age 16.

**Equality:** Ireland has equality legislation that prohibits discrimination
in [ employment ](/en/employment/equality-in-work/equality-in-the-workplace/)
and [ access to services ](https://www.ihrec.ie/guides-and-tools/human-rights-
and-equality-in-the-provision-of-good-and-services/what-does-the-law-
say/equal-status-acts/) on a number of grounds including nationality and race.

**Housing:** Some people live in [ owner-occupied private housing
](/en/housing/owning-a-home/) while a number live in [ private rented
accommodation ](/en/housing/renting-a-home/) . [ Social ](/en/housing/local-
authority-and-social-housing/) housing is available, but you generally need to
have been resident in Ireland for a period of time before you become eligible
for it.

**Health services:** If you are ordinarily resident in Ireland, you may be
entitled to a range of [ health services ](/en/moving-country/moving-to-
ireland/introduction-to-the-irish-system/health-care-in-ireland/) that are
either free of charge or subsidised by the Government. There is also a range
of private health care services. Your permission to stay in Ireland may
include a requirement that you have [ private health insurance.
](/en/health/health-system/private-health-insurance/)

**Social security entitlements:** If you are moving to Ireland you need to
know about your social security entitlements. EEA and Swiss nationals come
under the general rules on [ social security for migrant workers ](/en/social-
welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/claiming-a-social-welfare-payment/social-
insurance-contributions-from-abroad/) .

There are [ social security arrangements
](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/dc7910-guidelines-on-application-of-
bilateral-agreements/) with a small number of other countries. These generally
allow contributions in the other countries to be combined with your Irish
contributions to enable you to qualify for benefits.

**Social assistance payments:** Everyone, including Irish citizens, has to
meet the [ habitual residence requirement ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-
welfare-system/social-assistance-payments/habitual-residence-condition/) if
applying for a social assistance payment. Different rules apply to [ EEA
citizens ](/en/moving-country/working-in-ireland/migrant-workers/eu-citizens-
and-social-welfare/) .

**Pets:** There are regulations about importing pets from abroad, so you need
find out about the [ procedures for bringing your pet to Ireland.
](/en/moving-country/moving-to-ireland/coming-to-live-in-ireland/bringing-
pets-to-ireland/)

**Customs:** There are [ customs regulations about the importation of
prohibited or restricted goods ](/en/travel-and-recreation/travel-to-
ireland/customs-regulations-for-travellers/) and [ Customs and Excise officers
have the power to carry out searches of the baggage of people travelling to
Ireland ](/en/travel-and-recreation/travel-to-ireland/examination-of-
travellers-baggage-by-customs-and-excise-officers/) .

**Driving, cars and transport:** Full driving licences from all other EU
member states (and some other countries) are recognised for use in Ireland.
You should find out if you need to [ convert your driving licence to an Irish
one ](/en/travel-and-recreation/motoring/driver-licensing/exchanging-foreign-
driving-permit/) . If you want to bring your car to Ireland you need to know
about [ importing a car ](/en/moving-country/moving-to-ireland/coming-to-live-
in-ireland/importing-car-into-ireland/) and implications for Vehicle
Registration Tax (VRT).
