###  Workers posted to another EU country

You are [ a posted worker ](http://europa.eu/youreurope/citizens/work/work-
abroad/posted-workers/index_en.htm) when your employer sends you to work
temporarily in another EU country. You have certain specific rights as a
posted worker. If you are posted for more than a month, your employer must
inform you in writing, before you leave, of your pay and working conditions
while you are abroad.

You usually remain stay with the social security system of your country of
origin. You may pay income tax in that country as well but the situation
varies between member states. You can [ find out more about social security
for posted workers
](http://ec.europa.eu/social/BlobServlet?docId=11366&langId=en) or find [ more
information on Posted Workers below ](/en/moving-country/moving-
abroad/working-abroad/working-in-the-eu#lc3d02) .
