##  Your right to be treated equally

You have the right to be treated equally to Irish citizens and you should not
be discriminated against because you are not an Irish citizen. This means, you
should have the same rights as an Irish worker regarding:

  * [ Working rights ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/employment_rights_and_conditions/) and conditions 
  * [ How you are taxed ](/en/money-and-tax/tax/income-tax/how-your-tax-is-calculated/)
  * Social insurance contributions ( [ PRSI ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/social-insurance-prsi/paying-social-insurance/) ) 
  * Access to training 
  * Membership of [ trade unions ](/en/employment/employment-rights-and-conditions/industrial-relations-and-trade-unions/trade-unions/)
  * Access to [ education ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/education/) (including schools for your children) 
  * [ Access to healthcare ](/en/health/health-system/entitlement-to-public-health-services/)
