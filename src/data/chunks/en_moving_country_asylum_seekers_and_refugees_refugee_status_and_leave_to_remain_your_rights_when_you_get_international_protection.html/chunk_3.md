##  Rights of people with international protection

After you are declared a [ refugee ](/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and-
refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/refugees-asylum-seekers-
introduction#lad40a) or a person with [ subsidiary protection ](/en/moving-
country/asylum-seekers-and-refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/refugees-
asylum-seekers-introduction#l50016) , you have the right to:

  * Get a job without needing a work permit or any other permission 
  * Open a business, or work in a trade or profession 
  * Access education and training 
  * Get the same medical care and apply for the same social welfare and housing supports as an Irish citizen 
  * Live in Ireland for 3 years (which can then be renewed). Programme refugees may be granted permission to live in Ireland for a shorter period. 
  * Apply for a [ travel document ](/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and-refugees/refugee-status-and-leave-to-remain/travel-documents-for-refugees/)
  * Leave and re-enter Ireland (if you have a travel document) 
  * Apply for [ family reunification ](/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and-refugees/refugee-status-and-leave-to-remain/family-reunification-for-refugees-in-ireland/) for your family to join you in Ireland 

Refugees can [ apply for Irish citizenship ](/en/moving-country/irish-
citizenship/becoming-an-irish-citizen-through-naturalisation/) after 3 years
or residence. If you have subsidiary protection status or permission to
remain, you can apply after 5 years.
