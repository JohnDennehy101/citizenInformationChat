###  **Automatic backdating of payments**

The following payments can be backdated by up to 6 or 12 months, without you
needing a reason for applying late:

  * [ State Pension (Contributory) ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/older-and-retired-people/state-pension-contributory/) (6 months) 
  * [ Invalidity Pension ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/disability-and-illness/invalidity-pension/) (6 months) 
  * [ Widow’s, Widower’s, or Surviving Civil Partner’s (Contributory) Pension ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/death-related-benefits/widowed-persons-contrib-pension/) (6 months) 
  * [ Guardian’s Payment (Contributory) ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/death-related-benefits/guardian-and-orphan-payments/) (6 months) 
  * [ Medical Care ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/disability-and-illness/medical-care-scheme/) (12 months) 

The [ Household Benefits Package
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/extra-social-welfare-
benefits/household-benefits-package/) can be [ backdated in some cases.
](https://www.gov.ie/en/publication/3bacea-operational-guidelines-household-
benefits-package/#appendices)

Further backdating beyond the set time will be allowed only if:

  * The reason you didn’t apply resulted from incorrect information being supplied by the Department of Social Protection, or 
  * The delay was due to your incapacity.
