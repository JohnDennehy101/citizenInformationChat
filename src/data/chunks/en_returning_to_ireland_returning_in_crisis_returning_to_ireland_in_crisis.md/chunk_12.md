##  Contact your local Irish embassy

If you’re in a crisis abroad, you should contact the local [ Irish embassy or
consulate ](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/embassies/) for [ consular support
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/moving-abroad/leaving-
ireland/diplomatic-supports/) .

You can choose to [ register your contact details
](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/citizens-registration/) with
the Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA). If necessary and possible, they will
contact you during an unexpected crisis, such as a natural disaster or period
of civil unrest.

You can also contact the DFA’s [ Consular Assistance Unit
](https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/overseas-travel/assistance-abroad/) in Dublin
by calling +353 1 408 2527 during office hours, or using [ their online
contact form ](https://www.dfa.ie/about-us/contact-us/contact-consular-
assistance/) . You can also call +353 1 408 2000 for out-of-hours emergencies.
