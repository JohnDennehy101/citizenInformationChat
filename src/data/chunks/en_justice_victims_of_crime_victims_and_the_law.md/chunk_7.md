###  Criminal Justice Act 1993

The [ Criminal Justice Act 1993
](https://www.irishstatutebook.ie/eli/1993/act/6/enacted/en/print.html) forces
the court to consider the effect of a violent or sexual offence on the victim
when it is deciding the [ sentence ](/en/justice/criminal-law/criminal-
trial/sentence/) .

If you are a victim of a violent or sexual offence, you are also entitled to
give evidence in court about how the crime affected you.

The Act allows the Director of Public Prosecutions to appeal lenient
sentences. It also gives the court the power to force the offender to pay
compensation to the victim for any personal injury or loss suffered.
