###  **Medical report form**

You will need a Driving Licence Medical Report Form if you are applying for
driving licence categories C, C1, D, D1, CE, C1E, DE or D1E (buses and
trucks), unless you previously provided a medical report which is still
applicable.

You will need a Driving Licence Medical Report Form if you are applying for
driving licence categories AM, A1, A2, A, B, BE or W (motorcycles, cars and
work vehicles) if you have a specific disability or condition (see application
form).

If you are 75 or over, you will need a certification of fitness to drive from
your doctor to apply for a 3-year or a one-year licence. You can read the [
medical fitness to drive guidelines (pdf)
](https://www.ndls.ie/images/PDF_Documents/NDLS_Sla%CC%81inte_&_Tioma%CC%81int_2022_WEB.pdf)
.

You can [ download the medical report form (pdf)
](https://www.ndls.ie/images/Documents/Forms/Medical_Report_D_501.pdf) (it
must be printed back to back on one page). A registered medical practitioner
should complete your medical examination and then complete the form. You must
sign the declaration on the medical report form in the presence of the
registered medical practitioner. It must be submitted within 1 month of the
medical examination.
