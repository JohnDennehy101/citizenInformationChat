###  Income tax, USC and PRSI

Income tax rates will stay the same (at 20% and 40%), but there will be
increases to tax credits and changes to the income tax bands in 2024.

The [ standard rate tax band ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/money-
and-tax/tax/income-tax/how-your-tax-is-calculated/) (the amount you can earn
before you start to pay the higher rate of tax) will be increased by €2,000 to
€42,000 for a single person, with proportionate increases for married couples
and civil partners.

The following [ tax credits ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/money-and-
tax/tax/income-tax-credits-and-reliefs/introduction-to-income-tax-credits-and-
reliefs/) will increase by €100 to €1,875:

  * Personal Tax Credit 
  * Employee Tax Credit 
  * Earned Income Tax Credit 

The ceiling for the 2% [ Universal Social Charge (USC)
](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/money-and-tax/tax/income-
tax/universal-social-charge/) band will increase by €2,840 from €22,920 to
€25,760 from 1 January 2024. The 4.5% USC rate currently applies to income
between €22,900 and €70,044 will be reduced to 4% from 1 January 2024. The 4%
rate will apply to income between €25,761 and €70,044.

[ All PRSI rates ](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/social-welfare/irish-
social-welfare-system/social-insurance-prsi/paying-social-insurance/) will
increase by 0.1% on 1 October 2024.
