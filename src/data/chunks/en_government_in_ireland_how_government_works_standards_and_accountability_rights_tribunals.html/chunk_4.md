##  Examples of rights tribunals

Some well-known rights tribunals include:

  * [ Criminal Injuries Compensation Tribunal ](/en/justice/victims-of-crime/compensation-for-victims-of-crime/) : This tribunal decides the level of compensation to be paid to persons who have suffered injuries in the course of a criminal act. 
  * [ Workplace Relations Commission ](/en/employment/enforcement-and-redress/workplace-relations-commission/) : This tribunal investigates disputes between employers and employees and makes recommendations. 
  * [ International Protection Appeals Tribunal ](/en/moving-country/asylum-seekers-and-refugees/the-asylum-process-in-ireland/if-your-application-for-refugee-status-is-refused/) : This tribunal hears appeals by asylum seekers against decisions to refuse them international protection. 
  * [ An Bord Plean√°la ](/en/housing/planning-permission/appealing-planning-permission-decision/) : This tribunal hears appeals against either the granting or the refusing of planning permission by a planning authority. 
  * [ Social Welfare Appeals Office ](/en/social-welfare/irish-social-welfare-system/social-welfare-appeals/) : The appeals officer decides on applications for allowances or benefits under the social welfare legislation. 
  * [ Valuation Tribunal ](http://www.valuationtribunal.ie/) . This tribunal hears disputes in relation to the rateable valuation of properties. The rateable valuation is the value that the local authority puts on property for the purpose of deciding what rates will be charged. 
  * [ The Intellectual Property Office of Ireland: ](https://www.ipoi.gov.ie/en/) decides whether certain inventions are entitled to be registered as patents and whether certain symbols, drawings, marks and shapes may be registered as trade marks.
