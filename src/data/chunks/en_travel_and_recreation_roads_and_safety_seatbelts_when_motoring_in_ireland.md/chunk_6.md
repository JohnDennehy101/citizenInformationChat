###  Choosing and fitting a child car seat

All Children under 150 centimetres in height or weighing less than 36
kilograms (79lbs) must use a child restraint system suitable for their height
and weight while travelling in a car or goods vehicle (other than a taxi). You
can get more detailed information on [ choosing and fitting a child seat on
the RSA website ](https://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-
users/passengers/children/child-seats) . The RSA lists participating retailers
in their [ Code of Practice for Child Car Seat retailers (pdf)
](https://www.rsa.ie/docs/default-source/about/code-of-practice-for-child-car-
seat-retailers.pdf?Status=Master&sfvrsn=221c35f7_3) .

You must ensure that your child is in the correct car seat for their height or
weight. The weight range is indicated on the car seat or booster. The [ RSA
has a free, expert check it fit service ](https://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-
users/passengers/children/check-it-fits) available for parents.

You must de-active the front air bag if using a rear-facing car seat. Car
seats and boosters must meet EU or UN standards. See the [ FAQs about child
safety in vehicles on the RSA website ](https://www.rsa.ie/road-safety/road-
users/passengers/children/faqs) .

When travelling by bus, passengers aged 3 or over must wear seat belts, where
they are fitted.

You can get more detailed information on [ seat belts and child restraints
from the Road Safety Authority (RSA) website ](https://www.rsa.ie/road-
safety/road-users/vehicle-components/vehicle-components/seatbelts-and-child-
restraints) .
